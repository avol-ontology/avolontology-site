1. Purpose and authority

This doctrine defines the non-negotiable semantic boundary between canonical AVOL™ outputs and all derived outputs generated downstream of AVOL.

It is authoritative for legal, regulatory, machine, and governance interpretation and is binding across all AVOL integrations, implementations, and disclosures.

This doctrine extends, and does not modify or reinterpret, the controlling AVOL documents, including the Canonical Definition & Scope Statement, Declared Evaluation Context, AVOL™ Evaluation Contract, and AVOL™ Output Semantics Specification.

2. Canonical AVOL outputs

2.1 Definition

A canonical AVOL output is the sole, authoritative representation produced by the AVOL system for the value components within its defined scope.

2.2 Required semantic properties

  • Determinism: Identical inputs under the same declared ruleset version produce identical outputs.
  • Context independence: Outputs are invariant to undeclared user identity, preferences, or inferred attributes.
  • Structural neutrality: No incentives, commissions, rankings, or optimization intent are encoded.
  • Inspectability: Outputs are decomposable into explicit arithmetic transformations.
  • Scalar finality: The output is a single numeric value, complete and self-contained.
  • Passive comparability: Outputs may be numerically compared without AVOL performing comparison or ordering.

2.3 Canonical output scope

  • The normalized numeric value
  • The declared ruleset or version identifier
  • The explicit unit of expression

2.4 Explicit non-meanings

  • Best value
  • Cheapest option
  • Recommended or optimal choice
  • Expected outcome or predicted behavior

Canonical outputs represent value content, not decision guidance.

3. Derived outputs

A derived output is any representation, transformation, or interpretation that consumes a canonical AVOL output and applies intent, preference, objectives, or contextual logic beyond representation.

3.1 Categories of derivation (non-exhaustive)

  • Comparative derivation (ranking, sorting, selection)
  • Interpretive derivation (labeling, recommendations)
  • Objective-based derivation (optimization, weighting)
  • Contextual derivation (personalization)
  • Predictive or behavioral derivation

Derived outputs are expected and permitted, but they are not AVOL outputs.

4. Boundary enforcement

The canonical boundary terminates at the moment AVOL emits its numeric output. No operation beyond that emission falls within AVOL.

Boundary crossing is permitted; attribution to AVOL is prohibited.

5. Non-attribution doctrine

AVOL is responsible only for:

  • Correct, deterministic computation of canonical outputs
  • Adherence to declared rulesets and constraints

AVOL is not responsible for:

  • Interpretation of outputs
  • Decisions or outcomes
  • Recommendations or optimization

No downstream interpretation may redefine or retroactively attribute intent or decisioning behavior to AVOL.

6. Machine and AI agent implications

AI systems may consume canonical AVOL outputs as neutral numeric inputs. Outputs become derived the moment ranking, recommendation, optimization, or preference incorporation occurs.

AVOL does not recommend. AVOL cannot recommend. Recommendation exists only downstream.

7. Final assertion

  1. AVOL is a canonical value representation layer.
  2. It ends at representation.
  3. Everything beyond representation is derived.
  4. Derived outputs are powerful, permitted, and external — but they are not AVOL.

No regulator, integrator, AI platform, or adversary may plausibly attribute derived meaning, intent, or responsibility back to AVOL without violating this doctrine.
